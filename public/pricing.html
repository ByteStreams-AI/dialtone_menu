02 -Questions you might have

01 · How payment works

When a customer places a phone order, DialTone texts them a secure Stripe Checkout link. They pay the restaurant directly through Stripe Connect. Funds settle to your connected Stripe account on Stripe's normal payout schedule.

DialTone takes a small platform fee off the top of each card order — the percentage shown in your tier above. We never touch the money; Stripe handles the split at the moment of payment.

What counts as a transaction

The transaction fee applies only to card orders placed through DialTone and successfully paid. Walk-in orders, phone orders paid in cash at pickup, and abandoned checkouts are not charged a transaction fee.

Reservations are not transactions and carry no per-booking fee — they're included in your subscription.

02 · Pilot program

The Pilot tier is a curated program for restaurants with a desire to see if DialTone is a good fit. After we demo DialTone, we will provide setup of your account. You pay a $50 setup fee for branding and your selected images. No subscription for 15 days; Stripe's standard processing fees and our 1.5% transaction fee still apply on card orders.

At day 16, pilots convert to a paid tier — Food Truck, Single Location, or Multi-Location — or off DialTone entirely, no hard feelings. Pilot spots require a short application so we can make sure we're a mutual fit.

03 · No-contract, cancel anytime

Food Truck, Single Location, and Multi-Location are month-to-month. Cancel and you keep access through the end of the billing period. We do not lock restaurants into annual contracts, and we do not charge setup or onboarding fees.

Enterprise contracts are bespoke and may include annual terms and SLA commitments negotiated in the agreement.

A $50 setup fee applies to all new accounts.

04 · Prices may change

The numbers on this page reflect our current rates for paying restaurants.

Material pricing changes will be announced at least 30 days in advance by email to billing contacts.
