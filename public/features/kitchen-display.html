Kitchen Display (KDS)

The order shows up where the food gets made.

DialTone's restaurant kitchen display system puts every order — from the phone agent, the app, the kiosk, or the counter — onto the kitchen screen the moment it's placed. Clear, itemized, timestamped tickets with a sound alert. No paper flying off the rail, no re-keying, no "did anyone catch that phone order?"

Real-time, with a sound you can't miss.

New tickets appear instantly and chime, so the line hears an order land even with their hands full. Each ticket shows items, modifiers, special instructions, and how long it's been up — the whole board at a glance.

  • Instant, realtime board — no refresh, no polling for staff
  • Audible new-order alert cuts through the rush
  • Timers show how long each ticket has waited

Tap to advance. The customer's kept posted.

Bump a ticket from New to Preparing to Ready with a tap. When it's ready, the customer can be texted automatically — no runner walking to the front to ask "is order 34 up?"

  • One-tap Preparing → Ready, optimistic and instant
  • Optional "your order is ready" text on Ready
  • Pickup, dine-in, and delivery tickets all in one flow
🔔Ready alert — coming soon

Hold and fire, by course.

For table service, hold a course and fire it when the table's ready — appetizers now, entrées when the plates clear. Lines start held and drop into the kitchen the moment you fire them, so a four-top's food comes out together.

  • Hold courses; fire them from the board when the table's ready
  • Held vs fired lines shown separately — no ambiguity
  • Seats and courses tagged for clean plating
🍽️Coursing view — coming soon

Right ticket, right station.

Food goes to the kitchen; drinks go to the bar. DialTone routes each line to the station that makes it, so the line isn't sorting through cocktails and the bar isn't buried under entrées. (Drinks get their own screen — see Beverage Display.)

  • Per-station routing — kitchen and bar independently
  • Each station sees only what it's responsible for
  • Dine-in table signals flow back to the floor
