Beverage Display (BDS)

The bar gets its own screen.

DialTone's beverage display system routes drink tickets to the people who pour them — on a dedicated bar screen, separate from the kitchen. Cocktails and pints don't wait in line behind entrées, and the line cooks aren't scrolling past a round of margaritas to find the next plate.

🍹 Bar display — coming soon

Drinks routed to the bar, automatically.

Every beverage on an order peels off to the bar screen the instant it's placed. The bartender sees a clean queue of drinks — nothing to sort, nothing buried under food tickets.

  • Beverage lines routed to the bar station on their own
  • Same realtime board and sound alert as the kitchen
  • Works for phone, app, kiosk, dine-in, and counter orders
📋Drink queue — coming soon

Food and drinks finish on their own clocks.

The kitchen bumps food; the bar bumps drinks — independently. A table's food can be up while the cocktails are still shaking, and each side signals ready on its own. The floor sees both, so a runner knows exactly what's waiting.

  • Independent completion for food vs drinks
  • Per-station "ready" signals surface on the tables board
  • Keeps the pass clear — drinks never clog the food line
⏱️Independent completion — coming soon

One more screen, zero more systems.

The bar display is the same DialTone board, pointed at the bar — no separate vendor, no second login, no reconciling. Turn it on for restaurants with a bar; leave it off for those without.

  • Per-restaurant toggle — on for bars, invisible otherwise
  • Same menu, same orders, same admin as everything else
  • Pairs with the Kitchen Display for full coverage
